# Academic Tone Guidelines for Digital Twin Simulation Module

## Writing Standards

1. **Objective Language**: Use third-person perspective and avoid personal opinions or informal expressions
2. **Technical Precision**: Use precise technical terminology consistently throughout all chapters
3. **Citation Requirements**: All claims must be supported by peer-reviewed sources or established research
4. **Clarity**: Write at Flesch-Kincaid Grade 10-12 level for accessibility to target audience
5. **Formality**: Maintain formal academic language appropriate for educational content

## Content Structure

1. **Learning Objectives**: Clearly state what readers will understand after each chapter
2. **Evidence-based Content**: Support all technical claims with research citations
3. **Balanced Coverage**: Provide comprehensive coverage of topics without excessive detail
4. **Logical Flow**: Ensure smooth transitions between concepts and sections

## Citation Standards

1. **APA Format**: All references must follow APA 7th edition guidelines
2. **Recent Sources**: Prioritize sources from the last 10 years
3. **Peer-Reviewed Priority**: At least 50% of sources should be peer-reviewed
4. **Consistency**: Use consistent citation format throughout all chapters
